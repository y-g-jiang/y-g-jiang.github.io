Software Overview

This software corrects for color casts in the deep shadows of digital cameras—an issue caused by inaccurate black levels when dealing with low-bit data. It achieves this by standardizing the DNG's shadow data against a precise floating-point black level, performing two floating-point scaling operations, rendering the result as an integer through a mature color pipeline and making a dithered fusion.

Special Notes

This software is designed to combat color casts in the shadows at low ISOs, which are caused by black level precision issues. However, some of the color cast in the shadows of full-frame sensors (especially after an 8+ stop push) originates from the shadow Read Noise (RN) itself, not from black level precision problems.

For most full-frame cameras, using my software can indeed provide a degree of improvement. However, it may not work as effectively for certain cameras (for example, some Canon models exhibit a strong magenta cast, a result similar to a direct decoding in ACR within Photoshop when color noise reduction and other sliders are set to 0, without clicking 'Open Image'). In such cases, the result often improves dramatically after applying color noise reduction during RAW processing.

If you encounter any issues, please inform me promptly, and I will carefully consider whether it is a problem with the software's decoding or a limitation dictated by physical principles.
